Topological-materials ML: match the symmetry, exactly

Classifying Chern phases of the Haldane model: the physically matched C3 prior wins at every scarce size, while the too-large C6 group — broken by the mass term — underperforms it everywhere. Built-in mismatch control; the law cuts both ways.

Materials ML bakes in E(3) or SO(3) symmetry, but nobody bakes in the honeycomb’s crystallographic C6/C3 point group — graphene’s own symmetry. That is an unclaimed corner, and a clean test of the equivariance law.
